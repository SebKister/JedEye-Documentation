# JedEyeLink - Data Transfer

The JedEye v2 is supported on Microsoft Windows(>7), Apple Mac OSX (>10.6) and Linux (Kernel >5.0.0)

Currently there’s no driver installation required for the JedEye v2 on any of the supported operating system.

JedEyeLink is the software that interfaces your JedEye v2 with your computer,
allows the download of the data as well as the visualization of the survey.
It also allows to adjust certain settings that are not available on the JedEye menu.

You can download the software [here](https://www.arianesline.com/mnemo/) 

## Connection ##

Connect the USB cable to the device as shown on the picture and then to your computer. The order is important.

![20221002_111940.jpg](placeholder.png){width=320}

The blue led indicates that the connection to the computer/charger was successful.

![ledindicator.jpg](placeholder.png){width=320}

Turn on JedEye. At that point your computer should have recognized the device.

## Launch JedEyeLink ##
You can now launch JedEyeLink application on your computer.

> If “JedEye not detected” appears in the right corner, make sure the connector is properly placed on the JedEye 
and the device is turned ON than press the refresh button in the middle of JedEyeLink's window.

If the connection is successful, you should have the serial number of your device displayed in the upper right corner.

The **Data** tab will give you options to _download_ and _save the survey data contained_ on
the JedEye as DMP and various other formats that you can use in your favorite survey software.

The **Setting** tab will give you access to different settings of the JedEye that are not accessible through the settings Menu.
Most of those are self explanatory. The ones locked are factory settings that should not be modified.
JedEyeLink will save the data on the device in an Excel sheet. You can then copy-paste it in your favorite mapping software.

The **CLI** tab is for advanced command line based interaction with the JedEye.
